# GTM.md

## Who is the exact target user

**Primary:** Engineering Manager or CTO at a Series A startup, 10–40 people, 4–15 engineers. They own the engineering budget line in a board-presented P&L. They approved the AI tool subscriptions 12–18 months ago when the team was half its current size and the plans made sense, but haven't revisited them since. They are not actively looking for savings — they're looking for reasons to stop getting asked about the AI budget in board prep.

**Secondary:** Co-founder/CEO at a seed-stage startup (3–8 people) doing the books themselves. They see Stripe invoices for Cursor, Claude, and ChatGPT and have a vague feeling they're paying for overlap but don't have time to audit it manually.

**Not the target:** Freelancers (single seat, low spend, low LTV for Credex). Enterprise CTOs (procurement processes mean they won't self-serve a free web tool). Pure non-technical founders (won't know what "plan" they're on for technical tools).

## What they Google or scroll through before they'd want this tool

- "cursor vs github copilot which is better 2025" (they're evaluating a tool switch, not yet thinking about spend)
- "how much should a startup spend on AI tools per engineer" (benchmark-seeking)
- "claude pro vs claude team" (in-the-moment plan decision)
- "chatgpt team vs plus difference" (exact same behaviour)
- Hacker News: anything with "AI spend" or "AI subscriptions" in the title
- X/Twitter: threads about AI tool costs from CTOs of companies they respect (Paul Copplestone, Guillermo Rauch posting about their stack)

The search intent is not "I want an audit tool" — it's "I want to know if I'm on the right plan." SpendWise AI has to intercept that intent, not wait for the explicit audit search.

## How to get the first 100 users in 30 days with $0

**Week 1: Direct seeding (target: 40 users)**

Post a genuine "what does your team pay for AI tools monthly" poll on X. Don't mention SpendWise AI. When people respond with real numbers, DM them: "Based on what you mentioned, I think you're on the wrong plan for your team size. I built a tool that does this analysis — want me to run your stack through it?" This is not spam; it's a targeted, relevant offer. Target 50 DMs → expect 10 conversations → 5–8 people use the tool.

Find 10 threads on Hacker News in the last 90 days where people discussed AI tool costs. Reply to the thread with a link: "Built a free tool for exactly this — [link]. No login, takes 2 min." HN replies to old threads still get seen. Target 2 quality replies.

Post in 3 specific Slack communities (Rands, OnDeck, one founder community): "Shipped something this week — a free AI spend auditor. It identifies plan mismatches and overlap. Honest feedback welcome: [link]." Genuine community posts (not spam) get engagement.

**Week 2: Amplification (target: 60 additional users)**

Ask the 8–10 early users to share their audit URL publicly. The shareable URL already strips personal info — make this easy. One viral HN post from a respected engineer ("Found $340/mo in waste in 90 seconds") does more than 50 cold DMs.

Write a post in the format: "We pay $X/month for AI tools. Here's what I found when I audited every tool by team size and use case." Include the audit URL for our own mock team. Post to X, HN Show HN, r/startups.
